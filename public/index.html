Timeline

1894

A&M established football team.  Compiled a 1-1 record in first season.  A&M plays it's first game against UT, losing 38-0

1909

Coach Charley Moran is hired.  A&M's first undefeated season

1914

Charley Moran leaves after compiling a 38-8-4 record.  Edwin Harlan is hired to replace him

1915

A&M joins the southwest conference along with Texas,  Baylor,  Arkansas, Rice,   Oklahoma,  and Oklahoma St.  (Oklahoma and Oklahoma St. would leave in 1919 and 1925 respectively)

1917

TAMU hires Dana X. Bible to be head coach.  Coach Bible led the aggies to an undefeated season in 1917.  Coach Bible would leave A&M for LSU.

1919

A&M rehired coach Bible.  His 1919 team went undefeated, outscoring opponents 275-0.  The 1919 team was named national champion.

1922

The 12th man tradtion is started when Tamu only had 18 players in a game against Centre College.  Players were starting to get injured so Coach Bible called on a reserve player in the press box named E. King Gill to suit up for the game,  thus the tradtion was born.

1927

The aggies win their 2nd national championships under coach Bible

1928

Coach Bible leaves Texas A&M for the Nebraska Cornhuksers.  Coach Bible lead the aggies to a record of 72-19-9,two national championships, and 5 southwest conference titles in 1o seasons as head coach.  Matty Bell is hired as his replacement

1934

Coach Matty Bell lead the Aggies to an 24-21-3 record over 5 years.  In 1934, coach Bell left for SMU, with Homer Norton hired as his replacement.

1939

In 1939 the Aggies went 10-0 during the regular season. The #1 Aggies would then beat the #5 Tulane Green Wave in the Sugar Bowl 14-13, giving the Aggies their 3rd and final national championship.

1947

Coach Norton is fired after a 3-6-1 season and 8 straight losses to the Longhorns.  During his tenure, Coach Norton compiled a record of 82-53-9, giving him the second most wins by a TAMU coach. Two of coach Norton's players, John Kimbrough and Joe Routt have roads named after them at Kyle Field.  Running backs coach Harry Stiteler was promoted as his replacement

1950

After a 7-4 season, Stiteler reported that he was assaulted in Houston.  Stiteler refused to speak to police and later revealed that he lied about the nature of the assault, so he resigned.  Raymond George would be his replacement, earning a 12-14-4 record in 3 years.

1954

Paul "Bear" Bryant is hired to be the aggies coach.  Bryant was coach for 3 years, compiling a record of 25-14-2.  After being told that TAMU would be the last team in the conference to integrate, coach Bryant left for his Alma Mater, Alabama.  At Alabama, Coach Bryant would win a then record 6 national championships (He would be passed by Alabama coach Nick Saban in 2020).

1958

John David Crow wins A&M's first Heisman Trophy.

1965

After 6 years of disappointing play (18-47-5 record) and 2 head coaches, the Aggies hired Alabama Assistant and Former Aggies Gene Stallings to be head coach

1971

Gene Stallings is fired after compiling a record of 27-45-1 in 7 seasons. Stallings only had 1 successful season in which the Aggies won the Southwest Conference and beat Alabama in the Cottton Bowl.  Stallings would eventually go on to win a national championship at Alabama in 1992.   Emory Ballard is hired as his replacement.

1978

Emory Ballard resigned.  Ballord's tenure was significantly better than Stallings, posting a 48-27 record and 3 top-15 finishes in 7 years.  Tom Wilson was hired as his replacement.

1982

Tom Wilson was fired and Jackie Sherril was hired as his replacement.   Coach Wilson compiled a record of 21-19 with 1 bowl win in 3 seasons.

1988

In 1988 the NCAA the Aggies were put on probation for 2 years due to a multitude of rules violations.  Jackie Sherril resigned as a result   During his tenure, Sherril went 52-28-1, led the Aggies to 3 consecutive Southwest Conference titles, and consequentially 3 Cotton Bowl apperances.  He also started the 12th Man Kickoff tradtion.  Defensive coordinator R.C. Slocum was promoted to Head Coach.

1991

The Aggies had the top defense in the Nation and had the best defense in the Southwest conference, which they would also have in 1992 and 1993.   This defense would be known as the Wrecking Crew.  

1995

The Aggies unbeaten home streak ended which ran from 1989 to 1995   R. C. Slocum would only lose at home 12 times in 14 years, making Kyle Field the hardest place to play in the 90s.

1996

Texas A&M, Texas, Baylor, and Texas Tech left the disbanding Southwest conference and formed the Big 12 conference with Colorado, Nebraska, Missouri, Oklahoma, Oklahoma State, Iowa State, Kansas State, and Kansas.  The Aggies would win a Big 12 Championship in 1998 and 2 Big 12 south Titles in 1997 and 1998.

2002

R.C. Slocum Resigned  During his tenure, he compiled an 123-47-2 record and was the fastest active coach to reach 100 wins at the time.  He was the winningest coach in Aggies history, had 50 drafted players, won Southwest conference coach of the year 3 times, and never had a season below .500.  Dennis Franchione was hired as his replacment.

2007

Dennis Franchione resigned after it was discovered that he was selling a email newsletter, which is not allowed under NCAA rules.  Coach Franchione had a 32-28 record after 5 seasons. Houston Texans Offensive coordinator Mike Sherman was his replacement.

2011

Mike Sherman was fired.  He had a 25-25 record in 4 seasons and only 1 winning season.   Sherman coached current Tennessee Titans quarterback Ryan Tannehill and Los Angeles Rams Linebacker Von Miller during his tenure.  U of H coach Kevin Sumlin was hired as replacement 

2012

The Aggies left the Big 12 for the SEC, ending A&Ms rivalry with Texas (which will be resumed in 2025 when Texas joins the SEC).  The Aggies went 11-2 and beat No. 1 Alabma and No. 11 Oklahoma.  Aggies quarterback Johnny Manziel became the first freshman Heisman winner

2017

Kevin Sumlin was fired after a 7-5 record  Sumlin was 51-26 during his 6 year tenure. Sumlin coached Browns DE Myles Garret during his tenure. Florida State head coach and 2013 National championship winner Jimbo Fisher was hired as his replacment.

2025

Texas and Oklahoma will join the SEC, resuming the Lone Star Showdown.
